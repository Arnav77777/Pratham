About the Event

Our event is designed to bring together industry professionals, students, and enthusiasts to discuss the latest trends in the field.

Competition Format

Rules

• General Rules: 1. Eligibility: o Teams may consist of students from different departments or colleges. 2. Team Formation: o Teams can have up to 2 members. o Team members must be registered before the start of the hackathon. o Each participant can only be part of one team. 3. Registration: o All participants must register online by the deadline. o Provide valid contact information and team details during registration. 4. Entry Fee: o The entry fee is ₹100 per participant, regardless of whether the team consists of 1 or 2 members. Each participant must pay ₹100. 5. Event Duration: o The hackathon runs from 9 AM to 5 PM on the scheduled day. o Participants must adhere to the event schedule and submit their projects before the 5 PM deadline. • Project Requirements: 1. Technology Stack: o Projects must be developed using the specified technologies (e.g., HTML, CSS, JavaScript, or any frameworks for web development). o Use of official documentation is allowed. o External libraries and frameworks are allowed but must be cited. 2. Originality: o Projects must be original and created during the hackathon. o Reusing code or projects from previous hackathons or other sources is not allowed. 3. Prohibition on AI Tools: o The use of any AI tools or platforms for project development is strictly prohibited. All code must be written manually by team members. 4. Submission: o Submit the final project code by the given deadline. o Projects should be submitted through the provided submission platform or as instructed. • Conduct and Fairness: 1. Collaboration: o Teams are encouraged to collaborate and help each other but must work within their own team. o External assistance or consulting with non-team members during the event is not allowed. 2. Judging Criteria: o Projects will be judged based on criteria such as innovation, functionality, design, creativity, usability, and adherence to the problem statement. o Judges’ decisions are final and binding. 3. Code of Conduct: o All participants must adhere to a code of conduct, including respectful communication and behavior. o Any form of cheating or plagiarism will result in disqualification. • Logistics: 1. Resources: o Participants will have access to necessary resources, including internet (Ethernet connections), workspaces, and any provided tools or APIs. o Ensure to bring your own laptops and other necessary equipment. 2. Support: o Mentors and technical support will be available throughout the event. o Reach out to support staff for any technical issues or questions. • Post-Event: 1. Presentation: o Teams will have the opportunity to present their projects to the judges. o Each team will have a designated time to showcase their work and answer questions. 2. Prizes and Recognition: o Winners will be announced and awarded prizes based on the judges' evaluation. o Certificates or other forms of recognition will be given to all participants.
